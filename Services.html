LogMeIn

LogMeIn’s corporate social responsibility (CSR) program, Mission Possible, unlocks the potential of our employees, products, and culture to create long-term, sustainable social and environmental impact that positively affects change in the world and improves peoples’ lives. We focus our social impact on two main areas: education & youth programs and critical human needs. In 2020, we also have rolled out sustainability programs at both the corporate and individual level, allowing us to make an operational impact on the environment.

Guidewire Software

Our community impact program is titled Guidewire Gives Back, and encourages local employee engagement with the community with 3 VTO days per year and a matching gift program of up to USD$1000 per year. Guidewire strives to support the communities where our employees live and work with their time, effort, and finances.

Instacart

Our social impact program, Instacart Serves, aims to nourish our communities by helping to remove barriers to food access and enrich the lives of our communities through an annual volunteer week of service, grocery donations, disaster relief, and giving campaigns. The Instacart Serves program focuses support across three main areas: food insecurity, supporting traditionally marginalized communities, and disaster relief.

CSAA Insurance Group, a AAA Insurer

At CSAA Insurance Group we provide grants to nonprofits across the country and support employees with their own involvement through volunteerism and giving programs. Over the years we have built a pro bono volunteerism program that connects HR, Marketing, and Legal expertise to nonprofit partners. In response to COVID-19, we connected our café staff to provide meals to local nonprofits. We continuously seek to leverage all our assets to do good in our communities.

Smartsheet

In June, our employees nominated Black Lives Matter as the recipient organization for a US$150,000 corporate donation from Smartsheet. Additionally, when we launched Smartsheet Cares, our corporate giving platform, later in the summer, we did so with a 2:1 matching campaign (up to US$150,000) for additional employee-nominated organizations in support of Black Americans. We also offer discounted pricing to nonprofits interested in using Smartsheet for their organizations.

Hollister Staffing

Community is the heart of Hollister. It’s our compass and it is what fueled Kip’s, our CEO’s, passion for founding the business 32 years ago. This past year, we focused on co-creating the programs that are part of RecoveryWorks – a non-profit program at Massachusetts General Hospital, an affiliate of Harvard Medical School, dedicated to connecting individuals who have substance use disorders with employment opportunities and matching their level of experience and skill sets. When COVID-19 hit Boston in March, we developed a series of virtual programs – free of charge – designed to support our community, bring leaders together, and enhance and deepen human connection. We developed over 16 program offerings offered in excess of 65 free sessions via Zoom with over 750 registrations, including weekly guided meditations led by Kip. Kip also started a podcast during this time, Well Being Walks, that is accessible through multiple channels.
